Creating the path

The way we organise ourselves is just as important as the way we organise our systems and our code. When leading I've found that small, highly collaborative teams are the most effective.

I encourage conversations in my team, listening to all ideas, taking advice, and having open discussions about the way forward. All of the teams that I have led have increased the speed, quality, and ambition of their work. This is all while feeling positive and that their work makes a real difference.

More experience than you can shake a stick at

I've had the privilege to work in a range of companies in varied industries. I've created outstanding software aimed at wildly different audiences, from a single user to millions of concurrent users. Throughout the numerous projects I've worked on, I've gained experience in designing and implementing a wide variety of different architectures. I embrace building modular components that are reusable, extensible, and easy to test.

Often the most difficult question to answer is "What do you want to see and do?". So throughout development I work closely with users to figure out what they need, demonstrating current work and eliciting feedback regularly.
